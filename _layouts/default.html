Jaeeon Lee

I am a Postdoc in Bernardo Sabatini lab at the Harvard Medical School (moving to Rui Costa Lab) I joined the Ph.D program in Neurobiology in 2015, and obtained my Ph.D in the Sabatini lab in 2021. Before, I obtained my Bachelor's degree in Bio-engineering at KAIST in 2015. As an undergrad, I previously worked in Gerstner's lab, Wickens' lab, Medina lab and Jung's lab.

I am broadly interested in circuit mechanisms of computations relevant for decision making. During my Ph.D, I investigated 1) Topography within mouse Basal Ganglia, 2) How the Basal Ganglia-Collicular circuit can drive exploration. I have experience with anatomical tracing, optogenetics, in vivo extracellular recording, and data analysis using machine learning.

Work during my Ph.D in Sabatini lab

Striatal indirect pathway mediates exploration via collicular competition

Jaeeon Lee*

, Bernardo Sabatini
bioRxiv (accepted),

pdf

We dissected the functional impact of stimulating the striatal D2 indirect pathway, on behavior and neural dynamics in a downstream region (colliculus). We show that D2 pathway can both suppress and trigger movement on the contra/ipsi side respectively. This can be explained by paradoxical excitaiton of contralateral SC. We propose a general circuit mechanism for action switching mediated by basal ganglia-collicular circuit.

Anatomically segregated basal ganglia pathways allow parallel behavioral modulation

Jaeeon Lee*

, Wengang Wang, Bernardo Sabatini
Nature Neuroscience, in press,

pdf

We show topographical loops starting from striatum to Pf, VM and SC in the mouse brain. Segregated pathways in medial vs ventrolateral striatum modulate turning/licking behavior respectively, confirming parallel organization of BG output pathways.
